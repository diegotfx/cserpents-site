Rack Setup
Do I need a thermostat?+

Yes — always. Never plug a rack directly into an outlet. The flexwatt heat tape does get hot, and without a thermostat you run the risk of overheating your animals. A thermostat is non-negotiable.

How do I know my tub temperatures are correct?+

Use a digital temp gun. Check the heated area of each tub before introducing any animals. Your thermostat is a guide — the temp gun gives you the actual reading.

Where do I place the thermostat probe?+

For hatchling and shoebox racks, place the probe directly on the aluminum cover or the heat tape. For all other racks, place the probe on the heat tape of the middle shelf. You can run the wire through the front of the rack or carefully cut a small opening in the back panel.

Can I use one thermostat for multiple racks?+

Yes — but only for the same type of rack, and only up to the wattage capacity of your thermostat. For example, you can run up to 6 Sub-Adult V-28 racks on a single Vivarium VE-200 (700W). You cannot run two different rack types on the same thermostat unless using a dual-probe unit like the Vivarium VE-300 2X.

Do I need to drill holes in the tubs?+

Our racks are engineered with enough gap for adequate airflow. Holes are only necessary if you have a persistent condensation problem. Every environment is different — some keepers never drill, some do.

Can I stack my racks?+

Yes. Our racks stack with the same type of rack (two V-18s, two Sub-Adults, etc). Dowel pins are included. Only 2 dowels are needed — place them in opposite corners or both front/back. Only stack the same rack type.

Shipping & Orders
How long does it take to receive my order?+

Approximately 3 weeks from the time of order. This may be slightly shorter or longer depending on show schedule, order volume, or supply chain. We appreciate your patience.

My rack arrived with minor scratches. What should I do?+

Minor surface scratches can be covered with a small amount of black shoe polish. These don't affect the performance of your rack.

The back screws weren't installed. Is something missing?+

Back screws are removed for shipping. They can be re-installed with a standard Phillips head screwdriver. Hand tighten only — no power tools needed.

Thermostats
What's the difference between on/off and proportional thermostats?+

An on/off thermostat cuts power when the set temperature is reached and restores it when the temp drops. A proportional thermostat sends small pulses to maintain a steadier temperature. For rack systems, on/off works well. For incubators, we recommend proportional — more stable temps mean better hatch rates.

Which thermostat do you recommend?+

For most rack systems, the Vivarium VE-200 or VE-300 is the right choice. If you're running two different rack types simultaneously or pairing an incubator with a rack, the VE-300 2X gives you independent control of each system with its dual probes.
